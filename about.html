Conceptual Art Meets HTML & CSS Practice

The chromatic diet comes from a novel by Paul Auster, Leviathan. In it, an artist named Maria organizes meals by color and days by letter of the alphabet. Auster modeled the character Maria after a real artist, Sophie Calle.

Sophie Calle then adopted the role of the character based on herself. She ate Red on Tuesday, White on Wednesday, and lived a day in the letter 'B' (as in butterflies and Brigitte Bardot). Calle documented this experience in an artist's book, Double Game.
